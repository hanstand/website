Hannah Simmons | past projects

INNOCENT LOOKING PROBLEM,  2013

A study of 'movement paradoxes' and 'impossible tasks.' The interest was not in completing the tasks but in the methodology with which the tasks were approached. In what ways does the mover compromise to achieve the task at hand? What does the mover choose to hold on to? Does this reveal the very essence of the material?

ONLY RELATIVELY,  2013

The culmination of several investigative projects in which existing mathematical frameworks were translated into structures for developing movement material. An exploration of modularity and layering.

PENDING EQUILIBRIUM,  2012

A physical investigation of pendulums and simple harmonic motion. In particular, movers experimented with the ideas of equilibrium and isochronism: both maintaining these states and deviating from them. The differences between smaller and larger oscillations were examined. The performance was augmented by the installation of several large ceramic pendulums. The physical existence of the pendulums allowed dancers to continue direct experimentation onstage.

CORRIDOR,  2012

A play upon deterministic dynamical systems. Inspired by the debate over the fundamental nature of things: if our actions are inevitable, is movement material being discovered rather than created? Further inspired by the deterministic impulse to collapse past, present, and future into a single continuum; the need to do away with time. The work transpires in a world in which past, present, and future have become the same: they are equally knowable, they hold the same weight.
